# Paper Kit: how the tabletop version maps to the digital game

The paper prototype is generated from the same `data/*.json` files as the digital game.
One content source, two tables: fix a card once and both versions get it. This file
documents exactly what the paper kit simplifies and why, so playtest findings can be
read back into the digital design honestly.

## Scale conversion

Digital quantities live on a 0 to 1000 integer scale. Paper tracks run 0 to 20.
One paper step = 50 digital points. Every card effect in the data is a multiple of 50,
so cards always print as clean integers ("Trust +1", "Rival Capability +2").
The print script refuses to build a kit if a card value is not divisible by 50.

## Time

|             | Digital                         | Paper                         |
| ----------- | ------------------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| Turns       | 16                              | 10                            |
| One turn is | a quarter                       | roughly half a year           |
| Window      | 2026 Q3 to 2030 Q2              | mid 2026 to 2031              |
| Election    | turn 8                          | turn 5                        |
| Eras        | early 1-5, mid 6-10, late 11-16 | early 1-3, mid 4-7, late 8-10 |

Event timing on paper uses ERA decks only (three stacks, marked card backs). The finer
`turnMin`/`turnMax` windows are digital-only. Card conditions (flags, rival posture)
apply in both versions and are printed on the card.

## What carries over unchanged

- The 8 resources, the two society tracks, rival trust and substitution tracks.
- Every event card and policy card: costs, gates, effects, delayed effects, flags.
- The race track with the fog zone at 16+ (800+ digital), two capability markers.
- Worldview presets: each preset is a different hidden-dice lookup table in the envelope.
- The five endings and their spirit; thresholds are printed on the reference card.

## Delayed effects: the tuck rule

When a card schedules a future effect, tuck the card half under the turn track at the
turn it comes due, delayed line showing. You can always see the bite coming, you just
cannot do much about it anymore. Policy cooldowns use the same rule: tuck the policy
sideways N slots ahead, it returns to the rack when the marker reaches it.

## Flags

The board has a flag row of named checkboxes (Open Weights World, Export Crackdown,
Treaty Channel, Natsec Merged, Crash Program, Evals Public, Evals Classified,
Weights Stolen, Grid Priority, Incident Dismissed). Cards that need a flag say so.

## Hidden information on paper: the envelope

At setup, WITHOUT looking, roll d10 twice, write the two digits on the slip, seal it in
the envelope (Alignment Difficulty first, Takeoff Steepness second). The preset's lookup
table maps the digits to bands only when the envelope is opened.

- Mid-game, nobody knows the truth. Eval reports give you a band, never a value: the
  reference card maps your Safety Insight to a band width (Insight 0 = width 8, narrowing
  by 1 per 5 Insight, never below 2). Wide band means you are deploying blind.
- Incident checks (some events) roll d10 against printed thresholds. Uncertainty stays live.
- The envelope opens exactly once, at game end (or the moment a capability marker enters
  the fog zone endgame). Alignment resolution: Safety Insight versus difficulty times two,
  plus printed modifiers from cards like Crash Program. Then the ending resolves.

The commitment structure is the lesson: every deployment decision happens before you
learn the truth. That is takeaway 2, physically.

## Simplified subsystems (paper approximations of engine curves)

- R&D points per turn come from a printed lookup row (generated by sampling the
  `rndCapacity` curve and dividing by 50). Allocate whole points to Capability, Safety,
  Diffusion boxes each turn.
- Track growth per point allocated uses flat printed rates instead of the diminishing
  curves. Known distortion, accepted for v0; watch for runaway-capability feel in
  playtests before trusting it as a balance signal.
- Society updates and rival behavior run from small if-then tables on the reference card
  (checked top to bottom during World Update), not from the full curve set. The rival's
  three postures act with fixed printed moves per posture.
- Policies sit in an open rack: all eight visible and playable when their gates allow.
  The digital game rotates a smaller hand instead. Same cards, different access.
- Incident dice checks are REAL from v0.2: the kit ships an incident table page generated
  from data/incidents.json (same numbers as the digital game). The line below described kit
  v0 and stays for history: kit v0 used the d10s only for
  the envelope ritual.
- The hidden agency counter (Gradual Disempowerment hook) is digital-only. Cards that
  move it print without that line. The paper endgame ignores it by design.
- The satirical news ticker is digital-only. On paper the event cards carry the tone.
- Eval reports happen when you choose to read them (free action), not automatically.

## Not in the paper kit at all

- Seed sharing and replays (no RNG stream, dice are physical).
- The interactive debrief with counterfactual probes. The facilitation script covers the
  debrief conversation instead; it asks the same questions the digital debrief will.
- Autosaves, obviously. Pencil marks are the save file.

## Open tuning points for the playtest loop

1. Flat growth rates versus curve feel (see above).
2. Ending threshold exact values (first printed guesses on the reference card).
3. Whether 10 turns still lands the mid-game squeeze (turns 4-7) that the 16-turn
   digital pacing is designed around.
4. Ending precedence when several trigger in the same World Update (current order:
   Societal Breakdown, then Outpaced, then fog-zone resolution, then Negotiated Slowdown).
